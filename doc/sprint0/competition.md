**Overview**

Before starting development on our Library Management System, we analyzed several existing platforms that offer similar services. The goal was to understand how real-world systems handle features such as borrowing, reserving, and managing books, and to identify areas where our solution could improve the user experience. Our focus is on designing a simple, intuitive, and accessible system that benefits both regular users and librarians. The main competitors we reviewed were Toronto Public Library, York University Libraries, and Chapters/Indigo.

**Competitors**
1. Toronto Public Library (TPL)
 Website: https://www.torontopubliclibrary.ca/
 The Toronto Public Library is one of the most complete systems in Canada, offering both physical and digital lending options. It allows users to borrow and place holds on books online and provides access to OverDrive for eBooks. However, the website is not very beginner-friendly, its interface can feel overwhelming, and switching between physical and digital catalogues is confusing. Users also have limited personalization, such as recommendations or reviews.
2. York University Libraries
 Website: https://www.library.yorku.ca/
 YorkU Libraries are designed primarily for academic research and student use. It excels in accessing research databases, journals, and textbooks, but it lacks features for casual readers. Access is restricted to York students and staff, and there are no borrowing features for the general public. In addition, it doesn’t provide simple interactions like book reservations or a personalized dashboard for users.
3. Chapters/Indigo
 Website: https://www.chapters.indigo.ca/
 Chapters/Indigo is Canada’s largest commercial bookstore website. It offers fast browsing, personalized recommendations, and reviews from other readers. However, it is a retail platform, not a lending one. Users can only purchase items; there are no features for tracking borrowed books, returning eBooks, or managing user accounts for a library-style system.

**How Our System Is Different**

Our Library Management System combines the useful aspects of these competitors while adapting them for a smaller, more practical setup.

  - The system allows users to borrow and reserve both physical and electronic books through one unified interface.


  - The user dashboard is clean and minimal, so even first-time users can navigate it without confusion.


  - Librarians have their own dashboard to manage users, books, and overdue fines efficiently.


  - We also include user reviews, book recommendations, and “bookmark for later” features to make the system more interactive.


By focusing on usability and accessibility, our project aims to bridge the gap between academic and commercial systems, providing the simplicity of a bookstore interface with the functionality of a public library.
