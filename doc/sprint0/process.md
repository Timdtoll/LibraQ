**Reflect on how your team worked together.**  
The team got together in several meetings and did a portion of the work together. For each deliverable everyone contributed at least some ideas before all the team members split up to work on a couple deliverables individually. Then after some time the team would get together and everyone would share what progress they made. Overall it seemed to work well as everyone was able to finish their parts by the deadline and the workload felt pretty even. 

**How did you organize the team? Which tools did you use, if any?**  
One person would take initiative to start organizing a meeting and everyone would promptly pitch in on what date/time was best. During the meetings we divided the workload into even parts so that each member works on 2 deliverables and everyone works on [process.md](http://process.md). To choose who does what everyone said what they wanted to work on and we tried to keep in mind the relative difficulty of each deliverable, making sure everyone worked on one harder and one easier task. For team organization we only used the tools provided (Discord, Github and Trello).

**How did you make decisions?**  
Most decisions were discussed by the whole group in meetings, but some smaller decisions were made individually based on examples or common sense. We also tried to focus on stories that directly improve the user experience for core personas. Foundational stories were prioritized before additional features.  

**How frequently did you meet?**  
The group met three times during the 2 weeks of sprint0. Once to discuss what the project will be and complete [team.md](http://team.md) and other deliverables due by Oct 5th. Another time to discuss what needed to be completed by the end of sprint0 and assign the work, as well as briefly discuss what the contents for each part should include. Lastly, a couple days before all deliverables were due to go over each deliverable as a group and give any feedback or suggestions. 

**What lessons should you take forward to the next phase?**  
Meeting up to discuss what progress everyone has made a couple of days before the deadline was super helpful, but the meeting went on very long. Perhaps we should split into a couple shorter meetings with less things to go over.
