# Project Objective

Build a website for a public library through which members of the public are able to register for library accounts, search the catalogue, and check out or reserve books remotely. The system also provides a librarian portal for managing book availability and maintaining the catalogue to keep the website content up-to-date with the library's resources. 

# Key Users

**John Goodmann** is a 31-year-old office worker living in Canada. He enjoys reading on his two hour commute to the office. When he no longer has any more books to read, he drives an hour to his local library to browse for something new. Since John works in a technological field, he is very familiar with the internet and how to search for information online. He laments the fact that the current library website has no feature to reserve soon-to-be available books or check out books he is interested in from the website. If he had these features available to him, his trips to the library would be quicker and he would have more time to enjoy his hobby of reading.

**Myriam Morain** is a 40-year-old woman working at the local library in the suburbs. On her workdays, Myriam wakes up early to prepare herself for her work. Since the library is quite close to her home, her commute is quite short, something Myriam is quite glad about. Her responsibilities at work vary. Sometimes Myriam works at the front desk and keeps track of books that are returned or checked out, and other times she volunteers to read books to children and students who come to visit the library. Often these responsibilities require her to use the computer, something she is quite proficient with. However, the software the library uses is outdated and does the minimum required for keeping track of library goers and the books they check out. Myriam still has to make calls to people who have not returned books to charge them, as well as answer calls from people asking if certain books are available. While she loves working as a librarian, she still wishes for something that can take the burden off some of her duties, like automatically charging users for overdue books or a fast way to check, remove, and add books to the library’s collection. 

# Key Usage Scenarios

John Goodman is an avid reader who reads a lot of mystery novels. However, he’s read most of the popular titles already and isn’t sure what to read next. Using the library website, John searches for books by genre to discover new mystery titles. He browses the results, and checks out a book that is currently available. The website should make it easy for John to find and borrow books that match his interests, even when he doesn’t have a specific title in mind.

Recently the library got a small donation of books from a family that’s moving away. Myriam Morain, the librarian, has also noticed that over the years some of the books have been damaged and wants to take them off the shelves. She needs to be able to update the list of books the library displays online so that the public can rely on an up-to-date catalogue. 

# Key Principles

**User Accessibility and Intuitive Design**  
The design should prioritize clarity and intuitive navigation, ensuring that every interaction is straightforward and accessible for users of all ages and technical backgrounds, even if this means limiting visual complexity or advanced interface features.

**Maintain Reliable and Current Information**  
Book availability, reservations, and catalogue data should always stay accurate and synchronized between the public interface and the librarian portal to ensure trust in the system.
